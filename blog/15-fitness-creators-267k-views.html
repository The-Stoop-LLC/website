HOW 15 FITNESS CREATORS DROVE 267K+ VIEWS IN 2 CAMPAIGNS

#NewYearNewUs & #FallIntoFitness — X Shadyside × The Stoop

April 2026 · 10 min read

The Brief

X Shadyside is a boutique fitness studio in Pittsburgh's Shadyside neighborhood. In late 2022, ownership came to The Stoop with a clear goal: grow membership by putting the studio in front of the local fitness community — without relying on generic paid ads.

Our recommendation was an influencer-led approach, built around two distinct campaign windows tied to the studio's biggest membership drives: New Year (post-holiday resolutions) and Back-to-School / Fall (routine resets). Two campaigns, 15 creators total, $6,750 in combined spend.

Campaign 1: New Year New Us

Ran December 2022 through February 2023. We locked 7 creators with a focus on fitness instructors, chiropractors, and lifestyle influencers with authentic Pittsburgh ties:

  • Ashley Neicea ($600) — Fitness influencer & instructor, 79.6K followers
  • Dr. Brian Meenan ($1,000) — Chiropractor, 101K followers
  • Ana Sofia ($200) — Fitness model, 12.1K followers
  • Miranda Martin ($200) — Fitness influencer & instructor, 13K followers
  • Alex Nichols ($100) — Fitness model & instructor, 2.4K followers
  • Michelle Lagree ($100) — Registered dietitian, 1.4K followers
  • Cam Laconi ($100) — Fitness & lifestyle influencer, 12.7K followers

Each creator received a 1x 3-hour content shoot at X Shadyside, produced 1x Reel + 4x Story Posts, and granted 1 month of likeness rights. Total creator spend: $2,300.

Campaign 2: Fall into Fitness

Ran August through September 2023. With Campaign 1 data in hand, we leaned into slightly larger shoots and longer likeness windows. We locked 8 creators with stronger emphasis on content quality and multi-month usage rights:

Creator Sourcing Strategy

For Campaign 2 alone, we evaluated 25+ creators across fitness, wellness, and lifestyle categories before locking 8. Here's what we screened for:

  • Pittsburgh-local or strong Pittsburgh engagement — a national influencer means nothing to a Shadyside studio
  • Authentic audience — we checked engagement rates, not just follower counts
  • Non-compete status — several creators were eliminated because they were already partnered with competing studios
  • Willingness to commit to on-site shoots — user-generated phone videos perform worse than directed professional shoots
  • Reasonable rate relative to reach — we negotiated every deal; one major creator asked for $3,500 and didn't make the roster

The result: a roster where every dollar went to a creator who could actually move the needle for X Shadyside's specific market.

What We Learned

  • Micro-influencers often out-perform macros for local businesses. A $100 creator with 2.4K loyal Pittsburgh followers can drive more qualified foot traffic than a $600 creator with 80K scattered followers
  • Directed 3-hour shoots beat self-produced content every time. Professional creative direction during the shoot is where campaigns are won or lost
  • Stagger posts across 4–6 weeks, not 1–2. Sustained visibility outperforms a single burst, especially for membership-driven businesses
  • Negotiate longer likeness windows on repeat campaigns. Going from 1-month to 6–12 month usage rights in Campaign 2 gave X Shadyside an evergreen content library
  • Data carries forward. Campaign 1 gave us the benchmarks to price, pitch, and plan Campaign 2 more efficiently — and will keep compounding across future activations
